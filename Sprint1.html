Identity, Values and Strengths Exploration

Personal Values

My personal values can be summed up as constantly striving to embody the stoic virtues of fairness, kindness, courage, honesty and self-discipline.

Making Ethical Decisions: Values & Consequences

In my precious occupation I was employed by a large multinational corporation. During the course of my time there the general focus of the business shifted due to parent company management changes and as part of this shift found myself creating advertisements encouraging people into debt for items they most certainly did not need. I had some ethical concerns with this, and after some careful consideration decided to step away from the role. As a consequence of acting with authenticity and honour my departure went smoothly (which was not the case in several other such moves from within my team and here I am at Dev Academy

Who I Am: Strengths

Based off my INFJ-T Meyers-Briggs results I feel that the following Strengths would serve me well in both learning and career development. Creativity helps me solve problems in elegant ways and add beauty to the world. Being passionate and determined provides fuel for both the learning process and for getting things done in the most efficient way possible. When I am comfortable with a group of people I am also quite good at keeping morale up and in helping everyone get over the line with what is expected of us as a team.

Who I Am: Limitations

More problematic aspects of my personality would be that I can be very sensitive to criticism and that I tend to take it rather personally. This is something I am very aware of and I am constantly working on overcoming this issue. As a possible outflow of this sensitivity I also tend to be very private - this generally falls away to a certain extent once I am comfortable with the people around me but in a space dominated by extroverts this comfort takes a long time to achieve. A further issue that stands as the shadow side of Determination and Passion is that I can burn out easily as self sacrifice for the greater good is an incredibly normal way for me to behave.

Who I Am: The Hybrids

There are also a group of traits that could be both negative and positive, depending on how much wisdom I employ in such situations. I tend to be quite altruistic which can lead to me being taken advantage of or self sacrificing for the greater good. This does make me a pretty good person to have on team though, as I love helping wherever I can. In certain circumstances I can be pretty decisive and need to remain aware that while it can lead to getting things done, I also need to be open to changing the direction as the situation dictates. Perfectionism is also a two edged sword in that it can result in work of high quality, but left unchecked can make me a slow worker. Possibly the hardest trait to deal with is that I tend to need a cause to make my work meaningful, because without meaning I have no purpose and can become a bit unhappy about life in general.

Strengths and Values that can help me along the way

Passion and Curiosity: I am very curious and love nothing more than to dive into some subject that I am interested in and then to soak up as much of it as I can. This process is given extra momentum if I am passionate about the subject matter, as the added meaning this gives the learning process makes it easy to intuitively follow the information trail and incorporate relevant information into my frame of understanding. I believe that these two traits will serve me well when I hit an obstacle as my passion will provide the grit to keep going in the face of adversity while curiosity chips away at the edges with possible solutions.

Dealing with Conflicts

In my previous career there was often intense disagreement and resistance from other areas of the company. I realised that much of this stemmed from the caustic corporate culture, the pressure and the kind of personalities that were drawn to the positions I had to interact with. I found that the best way to handle difficult situations was to step away from them in a polite but firm manner in order to create some distance between the issue and the emotions bouncing around and then revisiting them with a kind and open heart once everything had settled down again. While I am asked to come up with other strategies I could use, I firmly believe that given the situation this is the best one available to me and I will continue to use it in ways tailored to suit each interaction.
